Cricket originated in England during the medieval period and gradually evolved from a simple village game into an internationally celebrated sport. The British Empire helped spread cricket to countries like India, Australia, Pakistan, South Africa, and the West Indies, where it became deeply rooted in culture. Major developments included Test cricket, One Day Internationals, and T20 cricket, each transforming the game’s style and popularity. Legendary players such as Sachin Tendulkar, Don Bradman, and MS Dhoni shaped cricket history. Technology, global tournaments like the Cricket World Cup, and women’s cricket have further expanded the sport’s reach and influence worldwide
